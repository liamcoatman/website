Correcting CIV-based Virial Black Hole Masses

At high redshift, $z>2$, black hole masses are normally derived using the velocity-width of the CIV broad emission line, based on the assumption that the observed velocity-widths arise from virial-induced motions. In many quasars, the CIV-emission line exhibits significant blue asymmetries ("blueshifts") with the line centroid displaced by up to thousands of km/s to the blue. These blueshifts almost certainly signal the presence of strong outflows, most likely originating in a disc wind (e.g. Richards et al. 2011). In Coatman et al. 2016a, we obtained near-infrared spectra, including the H$\alpha$ emission line, for 19 luminous SDSS quasars, at redshifts $2 < z < 2.7$, with CIV emission lines spanning the full-range of blueshifts present in the population. A strong correlation between CIV-velocity width and blueshift is found and, at large blueshifts, the velocity-widths appear to be dominated by non-virial motions. Black masses, based on the FWHM of the CIV-emission line, can be overestimated by a factor of nine at large blueshifts.

In Coatman et al. 2016b, MNRAS submitted, we present a sample of 249 quasars at redshifts $1.5 < z < 4.0$ and bolometric luminosities 45-47.5 erg/s with spectra covering CIV and one or both of the hydrogen Balmer H$\alpha$ and H$\beta$ emission lines. Part of this sample we collected on observing runs at the WHT and ESO NTT, part is archival spectra we have reduced, and part we have acquired through a collaboration with Joe Henawi (MPA, Heidelberg) and Gordon Richards (Drexel, USA).

We have generated an effective correction that allows BH masses, derived from rest-frame ultraviolet spectra, to be placed on an unbiased scale based on Balmer emission line properties visible in optical spectra. Our correction is easy to apply, as long as the quasar systemic redshift can be reliably determined. Hewett & Wild redshifts are already published for SDSS DR7 quasars, and improved redshifts from Allen & Hewett will soon be published for SDSS DR7/DR12. At this stage we will also publish our new BH mass estimates, which we hope will become the new standard.

Outflows & Hot Dust Emission in Luminous High-Redshift Quasars

Using large samples of quasars with photometric data from optical and NIR surveys, combined with spectroscopic data from the SDSS and BOSS surveys, we aim to parameterize and understand the relationship between the rest-frame UV and NIR properties, with a view to constraining the behaviour of outflows and the geometry of obscuring material surrounding the accretion disk.
